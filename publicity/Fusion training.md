Token is expensive. How can we save it?

One solution is to let LLMs reason step by step for hard problems, but answer directly for simple queries.

But what is the cost?

In our ACL 2026 SRW paper, Fusion Training for Mathematical Generalization in Large Language Models, we study the interaction between thinking and non-thinking training modes.

Our finding: these two modes can interfere with each other, while mixed training gives the best balance between mathematical reasoning and answer efficiency.

Paper: https://aclanthology.org/2026.acl-srw.64/

Authors: Congfeng Cao, Pengyu Zhang, Jelke Bloem
